April 1, 2026 - We are getting ready to celebrate Easter! We have an Easter morning breakfast pot luck, followed by an egg hunt for the kids. Then we have a rousing service and everyone goes home to celebrate the rest of the day with their families.

I love Easter Day! It is the day that Jesus rose again to new life, and made it possible for us to also live forever with Him. The Resurrection is a fact of history that simply can't be refuted. Jesus has Risen!
March 15, 2026 - What does the name "The Church of the Nazarene mean? Jesus, our Lord and Savior is the Nazarene. We are the people of Jesus - we are the church of the "Nazarene" Himself. We sometimes call ourselves "Nazarenes" but that is not strictly correct. We are of the same beliefs as THE Nazarene - Jesus. But we are not THE Nazarene. We are His followers.

The Church of the Nazarene is a holiness church in the Wesleyan tradition. We beleive in the same things that were taught by John Wesley and the Methodists of the 18th century. We take our holiness seriously, and we wish for all people to do the same. But we are not judgmental. We are saved by grace alone like every Christian.
February 21, 2026 - This little church is so cute! It was handmade by one of the original members of the church. We use it for our Alabaster offerings. Alabaster is celebrated in both February and October, and we take offerings and give it to the compassionate ministries arm of the Church of the Nazarene so that they can distribute it to those needing funds for buildings and other building projects all over the world!
February 1, 2026 - Five years today I began as pastor at Faith Fellowship. What a journey it has been! I love this church and all the people. The time seems to be going by so fast. Before I know it, it will be six years!

The crosses pictured here are on the wall of the fellowship hall. Over the decades people have brought in crosses that have personal meaning, and hung them on the wall. I hung one up about three years ago after much thought and prayer. Beautiful!
